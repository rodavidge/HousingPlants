Housing Plants

Some Houseplant History

Having plants inside a home goes back to the early Greeks and Romans. They grew plants in pots and sometimes brought them into their homes. You can see them depicted in paintings and sculptures. It became really popular in the 17th century when the author Hugh Platt in his book The Garden of Eden (1652) talked about the idea of cultivating plants indoors. Soon after, greenhouses and conservatories were being built in England to house exotic plants. In the mid 19th century, books were being written on growing plants at home included the use of greenhouses becoming more popularized (Graf, Byrd, Perrot, n.d.). What is a houseplant?

A houseplant is any plant that has been adapted to be grown indoors. There are certain types that are attractive and easy to take care of that have become popular to grow indoors. Some of the most common are exotic plants that can grow inside in colder climates even if they come from warmer areas of the world. Overall, most of them are native from tropical or near tropical areas (Graf, Byrd, Perrot, n.d.). Find out more on what a houseplant can look like in your home here.

Why should you get a houseplant?

There are a lot of benefits to having houseplants! The main thing of course is adding great decoration to your home but there are a few other things they can help out with. Indoor plants have been known to help reduce stress levels. It’s been found that indoor gardening lowers the stress response in people. They’ve even been known to be therapeutic by increasing feelings of well-being among people with depression, anxiety, and other conditions. Certain plants have even been know to improve the quality of air if you have a large number of them.
They’ve also been known to boost productivity. Studies have shown that students worked 12 percent faster and were less stressed with plants nearby. They can also help improve your whole outlook on work. People who have had plants in their office have found greater job satisfaction than those who didn’t (Joy, 2020). What to know before you get a houseplant

Getting your first houseplant can be pretty exciting, but there are somethings you should consider before doing so.

  • Pets and children: You always want to make sure that your houseplants are safe for both any pets like cats and dogs, as well as your children. There are several houseplants known to be poisonous to cats and dogs. If you have kids, you’ll have to keep in mind placement of the plants to make sure they don’t fall over on kids.
  • Sunlight: Different plants have different sunlight requirements. You want to make sure you have a room with the proper sunlight for the plants you’re trying to bring in.
  • Humidity: Depending on where you live, different homes can have different humidity levels. This is something to keep in mind when picking out plants because some more tropical plants may do well, but something like succulents might not.
  • Commitment: If you are a busy person, you might want to consider lower maintenance houseplants, but if you have a lot of time on your hands, you can consider higher maintenance plants.
